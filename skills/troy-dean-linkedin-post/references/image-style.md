# Image style — Troy Dean LinkedIn

The same rule that governs the writing governs the image: true over fake. Real over staged. If it looks like a stock-photo AI render with glossy lighting and a fake smile, it has failed.

This is a starting style. Troy can lock or change it any time. When he gives a new direction, update this file.

## Default look

- Clean and editorial. One clear subject. Lots of negative space.
- Muted, natural colour. No neon gradients, no glow, no lens flare.
- Looks shot on a real camera, not generated. Slight imperfection beats plastic perfection.
- Melbourne / studio / desk-and-work settings read true for Troy. Avoid generic corporate offices and handshake-over-a-city-skyline clichés.
- No fake charts, no fake dashboards, no invented numbers in the image.
- No staged "thought leader pointing at a whiteboard" poses.

## Two formats, pick by topic

1. **Bold text card.** Solid or near-solid background, one short line of text pulled from the post (the hook or the big idea). High contrast. Sentence case or all-caps only if it reads clean. This suits opinion and reframe posts.
2. **Real-feeling photo.** A single grounded scene that matches the post's concrete example. This suits story and proof posts.

## Hard nos in the image

- No hashtag text, no emojis baked into the image.
- No watermarks or fake logos.
- No em dashes or en dashes if text appears in the image.
- No bikini-billboard / bait imagery. Nothing that reads as desperate or tacky.
- No images of real named people unless Troy supplies the photo.

## Aspect ratio

- Default to 1200 x 627 (LinkedIn landscape) for link-style posts, or 1080 x 1350 (portrait) for text-card posts that need vertical room.
- Confirm with Troy if unsure.

## Prompt skeleton for the image tool

> [format: text card or real photo], [single subject / scene tied to the post topic], natural muted colour, editorial, lots of negative space, shot-on-real-camera feel, no gloss, no stock-photo gloss, no fake charts, no emojis, no hashtags. [text overlay, exactly this line, no em dashes:] "<hook line>".

Always show Troy the image with the post and wait for approval before anything gets published.
